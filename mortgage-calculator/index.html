üè¶ Conventional vs FHA Loans

    
            

            
            

    
    

        
‚úì Conventional Loan

        
        

            Best For:
            
    
    
  • Good to excellent credit (620+)
    • 
    
  • Larger down payments (5-20%)
    • 
    
  • Lower total costs long-term
    • 
    
  • Want PMI to drop at 20% equity
    • 
            

        

        
        

            Requirements:
            
    
    
  • Credit Score: 620 minimum (660+ recommended)
    • 
    
  • Down Payment: 3-20%
    • 
    
  • PMI: Required if <20% down
    • 
    
  • PMI Cost: 0.35-1.30% annually
    • 
    
  • PMI Duration: Drops at 20% equity
    • 
            

        

        
        

            Pros:
            
    
    
  • Lower interest rates
    • 
    
  • No upfront insurance premium
    • 
    
  • PMI drops automatically at 20%
    • 
    
  • Can use for any price range
    • 
    
  • More flexible loan terms
    • 
            

            
            Cons:
            
    
    
  • Harder to qualify (credit/income)
    • 
    
  • May require larger down payment
    • 
    
  • Stricter property requirements
    • 
            

        

    

    
    
    

        
üè† FHA Loan

        
        

            Best For:
            
    
    
  • Lower credit scores (580+)
    • 
    
  • Minimal down payment (3.5%)
    • 
    
  • First-time homebuyers
    • 
    
  • Lower income or savings
    • 
            

        

        
        

            Requirements:
            
    
    
  • Credit Score: 580 minimum (3.5% down)
    • 
    
  • Credit Score: 500-579 (10% down required)
    • 
    
  • Down Payment: 3.5-10%
    • 
    
  • Upfront MIP: 1.75% added to loan
    • 
    
  • Annual MIP: 0.50-0.75% for 30-year
    • 
    
  • MIP Duration: Usually full loan term
    • 
            

        

        
        

            Pros:
            
    
    
  • Easier to qualify (lower credit OK)
    • 
    
  • Only 3.5% down payment
    • 
    
  • More lenient debt-to-income ratios
    • 
    
  • Seller can pay closing costs
    • 
    
  • Good for credit rebuilding
    • 
            

            
            Cons:
            
    
    
  • Higher total costs (upfront + annual MIP)
    • 
    
  • MIP lasts for life of loan (usually)
    • 
    
  • Slightly higher interest rates
    • 
    
  • Property must meet FHA standards
    • 
    
  • Loan limits by county
    •

ü§î Quick Decision Guide

    

        
Choose CONVENTIONAL if:

        
    
            
  • Your credit score is 660+
    • 
            
  • You have 5%+ down payment saved
    • 
            
  • You want the lowest monthly payment long-term
    • 
            
  • You plan to pay down to 20% equity within a few years
    • 
        

        
        
Choose FHA if:

        
    
            
  • Your credit score is 580-660
    • 
            
  • You only have 3.5% down payment saved
    • 
            
  • You need more lenient qualification requirements
    • 
            
  • You're a first-time homebuyer with limited savings
    • 
            
  • Getting into a home NOW is more important than long-term costs
    • 
        

        
        

            üí° Pro Tip: If you qualify for both, conventional is usually cheaper long-term. However, FHA can get you into a home sooner with less cash needed upfront. You can always refinance to conventional later once you have 20% equity and better credit!
